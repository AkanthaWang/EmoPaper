# AffectNet Dataset Analysis

## Introduction

AffectNet is one of the most influential facial expression datasets in affective computing. It was designed to support recognition of facial expressions together with valence and arousal estimation in the wild.

## Data Collection Process

The dataset was collected from search engines using emotion-related keywords. This strategy helped gather images from unconstrained conditions, including variations in pose, illumination, background, and subject identity.

## Annotation Method

AffectNet uses manual annotation to label facial expressions and affective dimensions. The annotation pipeline combines human labeling with quality control to improve consistency across large-scale samples.

## Strengths and Limitations

Its major strengths are scale, diversity, and real-world complexity. These qualities make it valuable for training and benchmarking robust models. At the same time, the dataset inherits challenges such as annotation noise, class imbalance, and label ambiguity in natural scenes.

## Impact on Affective Computing Research

AffectNet substantially raised the bar for facial expression recognition in the wild. It became a common benchmark for comparing methods and influenced later work on robust training, label refinement, and multi-task affect prediction.
